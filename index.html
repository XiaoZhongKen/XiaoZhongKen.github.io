Hackers and Painters

发表于 2021-05-02 更新于 2021-05-24 分类于 读书笔记

我想通了一点，如果一个人因为中译本上译者的误读而愤愤不平，那么他只是在推卸责任。
为什么？如果他经常能发现译者的错误，说明他自己英文水平足以支持他阅读原文。可是又想偷懒，于是就去读中译本。在他眼里别人就必须为他提供正确无误的工作，这样自己就不用思考坐享其成，尤其是这个工作还是收费的。

总的来说，我们总是因为别人的错误而愤愤不平(因为利益相关)，而忽视自己一直也在这个错误的产生过程里，任其发展。总是抱有完美的外界条件是成功的充要条件的假设，这样就可以把失败的原因推卸给外部的不利因素上。否定自己在事情进展中可以发挥的积极作用。就好像一个有不在场证据的帮凶。

1. Intro

two most important skills author thinks are:

  • communication
  • Learning and Thinking

Programmers have to learn constantly—not just the stereotypical new technologies but also the problem domain of the application, the whims of the user community, the quirks of their teammates, the shifting sands of the industry, and the evolving characteristics of the project itself as it is built. We have to learn—and relearn— constantly. Then we have to apply this learning to the daily barrage of both old and new problems.
It sounds easy enough in principle perhaps, but learning, criti- cal thinking, creativity, and invention—all those mind-expanding skills—are all up to you. You don’t get taught; you have to learn. We tend to look at the teacher/learner relationship the wrong way around: it’s not that the teacher teaches; it’s that the student learns. The learning is always up to you.
